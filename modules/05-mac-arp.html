MAC & Local Networking

Layer 2 is where digital logic meets physical hardware. To secure a LAN, you must master how frames are handled before they ever reach a router.

1. The MAC Address

A Media Access Control address is the hardware's unique fingerprint. While IP addresses change based on the network, the MAC is (usually) permanent and burnt into the NIC.

// MAC Address Structure
00:1A:2B : 3C:4D:5E
[-- OUI (Vendor ID) --] [-- NIC Specific --]

By looking up the first 3 bytes (OUI) of a MAC address, hackers can identify the manufacturer (e.g., Apple, Cisco, or a specific IoT vendor) and look for known vulnerabilities in that hardware.

2. ARP Fundamentals

Launch ARP Visualizer

ARP (Address Resolution Protocol) is the glue between Layer 3 (IP) and Layer 2 (MAC). It functions on a "shout and listen" model.

root@kali:~# arp -a
// Viewing the local ARP Cache
gateway (192.168.1.1) at 00:50:56:c0:00:08 [ether] on eth0
victim (192.168.1.10) at 00:0c:29:4f:8a:33 [ether] on eth0

üì¶ The ARP Packet

ARP packets consist of an Opcode (1 for Request, 2 for Reply), a Sender IP/MAC, and a Target IP/MAC. They are trust-based; computers update their cache based on replies they never even asked for.

5. L2 Attack Surfaces

Because Layer 2 was designed for trust, it is highly susceptible to impersonation and flooding attacks.

ARP Spoofing (Man-in-the-Middle)

The attacker tells the Victim "I am the Gateway" and tells the Gateway "I am the Victim." Traffic now flows through the attacker's machine.

MAC Flooding

The attacker fills the CAM table with thousands of fake MAC addresses. When the table is full, most switches "Fail Open" and start acting like a Hub, broadcasting all traffic to all ports.

üìå Module Recap

  • MAC Addresses are physical IDs used for communication inside a LAN.
  • ARP resolves IP addresses to MAC addresses and is easily spoofed.
  • Switches use CAM tables to route traffic efficiently.
  • VLANs are the primary tool for logical network isolation.
  • L2 Attacks focus on sniffing data and breaking network segmentation.
